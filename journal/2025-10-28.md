# Simplification and continuous improvment/refocus/refactoring in PKM dev methodology

First, we specifically focused again today upon our review/refactoring/daily discipline for VS Code, to minimizing context-switchings builds upon using [essential VS Code keybindings](https://code.visualstudio.com/docs/configure/keybindings). This should go beyond simply mastering [Foam extension's VS Code shortcuts for note-taking](https://github.com/foambubble/foam/blob/main/docs/user/getting-started/note-taking-in-foam.md#keyboard-shortcuts) and the [recommended extensions for Foam](https://github.com/foambubble/foam/blob/main/docs/user/getting-started/recommended-extensions.md); for example, it may be good to occasionally peruse [materials on ***logsec***](https://github.com/logseq/awesome-logseq?tab=readme-ov-file#-guides-and-how-tos) or maybe things like [the Obsidian blog](https://obsidian.md/blog/) or to ask an AI for [recent BASB atomic note-taking refinements](https://grok.com/share/c2hhcmQtMi1jb3B5_3024d9b4-4e52-4c05-890c-6d4fd4ac1358) ... *FWIW, we are NOT fans of Obsidian; we don't understand why it has the mindshare it does...*

As with the use of any tool, [***daily*** disciplined use of **Foam** *is like a bathtub* -- **what you get out of it depends on what you put into it.**](https://github.com/foambubble/foam/blob/main/docs/user/index.md). Our own cognitive rewiring through daily notetaking is an especially important exercise -- we want to deliberately take some time to sharpen our own cognitive skillset with these most basic facets of how advanced toolsets are used. 

If one wants to optimize the machine-readability for automating notes in the future, it's necessary to to get practice with using things like using [note properties](https://github.com/foambubble/foam/blob/main/docs/user/features/note-properties.md), [templates](https://github.com/foambubble/foam/blob/main/docs/user/features/templates.md), and [graph visualization](https://github.com/foambubble/foam/blob/main/docs/user/features/graph-view.md).

In doing this we specifically want to avoid the AI soap opera and hypewars surrounding things like [AI unicorns](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scale_AI#Growth_(2019%E2%80%932025)) or the uber-pretentiosity of *end of world* or *singularity is near* AI benchmarks like [Humanity's **Last** Exam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Humanity%27s_Last_Exam) ... the inecapable lesson of History is that *there will always be something that's one million times bigger, better than the best we have now* -- five years from now, ***everyone*** *on average* will have 10X the information processing power they have available now -- some will have significantly more; in 10 years it will be 100X, in 15 1000X ... in 30 years, or one generation, it will be 1,000,000X ... the only certainty is that it'll continue indefinitely -- because it can ... thus, the *LAST*ness of this generation's exam is laughable, although EVERY generation believes that it is fighting THE War to end all wars.
