
As we [mentioned yesterday on Nov 6th]((https://github.com/AncientGuy/PKM/tree/main/journal/2025-11-06.md)), we do not want knowledge exploration that is merely random or blindly stumbling around ... we want a **navigable** map, which might portray things according to an ordering that might be changing and different -- but an ordering that makes some sense per what others care about in the world.

We probably want to incentivize our exploration, to make it more of a playful knowledge exploration game ... but to allow the incentives, weights to reflect changes that are happening.  

We're into **ludic** economics, like auctions, ways to accelerate the negotiation and build trust as in customer loyalty programs. By *ludic*, we mean those kinds of things that are engaging or relate to play or playfulness in a positive way, ie ***DON'T BE EVIL!!!*** We don't want customers, investors, employees to feel as if their companies, information systems and recommendation engines are playing games with customers/investors/employees as pawns OR are using *the game* as a way to get over on the customers/investors/employees on the humans ... it's increasingly important, because we all want/need more from a transaction than just the good / money exchanges in that transaction ... not just for the game of it ... there's more going on!

You don't exactly *need* to [delve into the Agentic Commerce Protocol (ACP) and the Shared Payment Token, co-developed by Stripe and OpenAI] ... for enabling things like the Instant Checkout feature in ChatGPT ... to understand *games* in goods, services, capital, data/compute, talent and how these markets morphing ... but *you don't wanna get played*.  So doing a weensy bit of a deep dive in that material probably helps a lot, when it comes to understanding the lay of the land ... you will want to go beyond just the [technical marketing overview developed by Stripe](https://youtu.be/l7r9jW2nEOI).

The reason that we want playfulness is NOT adding a bogus feature or a nifty twist or a stupid childish game -- instead, this game matters because it's about ***value, priorities, efficiency, and a degree of security*** for freedom from hassles of fraud ... thus about customer satisfaction, assured trust, reputation and loyalty ... beyond just seemingly silly "games" that used to be part of improving commerce and transportations like old school coupons or Gold Bond / S&H GreenStamps or frequent flyer miles or credit card loyalty points ... or even games that about catching game-cheaters or fraud detection of fraudsters who test strategies inside the normal, small transactions of repeat, trusting customers ... it's not JUST about repeat customers OR encouraging customer referrals or building good will and others things that drive order acquisition costs to near zero ... it is really about the much, much deeper game of customer loyalty ... how does the "game" satisfy the customer, ie make the customers/investors/employees feel like they are ["***winning***"](https://x.com/patrickc/status/1985468907747172552) relative to how persons would be doing if they didn't participate in the game.
