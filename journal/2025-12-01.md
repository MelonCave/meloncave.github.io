### Free and Open Source Audio Production Software

For free alternatives, open source tools provide robust options for recording, editing, TTS, and distribution without costs. 

While no single "Awesome" GitHub list covers *everything* for audiobook/podcast production, the [awesome-podcasting-tools](https://github.com/awesomelistsio/awesome-podcasting-tools) OR the [awesome-audio](https://github.com/DolbyIO/awesome-audio) repositories provide decent excellent starting point.  In the seemingly ***distant*** past, ie 2020 or so, I used to really count on these AWESOME GitHub repositories ... I thought these simple human-readable lists in MarkDown were one of the best things to come out of GitHub or crowdsourcing, but now I am not sure how well-curated these Awesome lists are any more in late 2025 ... doing this has sort of evolved, so that NOW **AI-assisted** quickie-curation of such repositories or collections of open source resources for the full pipeline (recording, hosting, analytics) is probably an activity worth doing, because the cost/pain/time of doing it is so trivial. 

What follows below is a highlighted top 10, chosen from GitHub repositories, focused on audio production ... I find that such ***top 10*** lists are useful in prompting AI to give me a quick-curated list of 100 or 200 similar/related items ... then the REAL ***fun*** begins with iterative refinement of search results and AI-assisted delving in a topic that I want to know more about ... don't worry that people like me are racking up monsterous power consumption doing this all day long ... FRANKLY, it's a lot cheaper and less resource-squandering than jumping in a vehicle, driving to a big box store like Menards or Home Depot or Lowes or all three and killing the better part of a day pushing a cart around gathering information on lumber or plumbing supplies ... and I don't have the patience, ambition, interest for doing many of these AI-assisted explorations, but the point is that, now I can do something like that and the resulting information that I get from iterative, successively-approximated querying is much better than the information that I could get 6 months or a year ago.
