The reason that cliches become the tired old cliches that everyone's heard 1000X before is that these things get suggested over and over for a simple reason: they have worked well, or well enough and reasonably consistently. 

AI or machine learning is really, at its fundamental atomistic core, about very tedious thinking ... where AI excels, it excels by applying trillions of simple, extremely tedious calculations in a bruteforce massive compute manner. AI, in particular LLM models, have gotten *good enough* results just by using randomness to build sentences with the most likely next word and paragraphs with the most likely next sentence ... but at it's core, this comes down to depending upon a random process.
