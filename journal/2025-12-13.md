# Gamified interactions from ludic economics to challenge assumptions

The POINT of this is about driving root cause thinking or about making that process almost fun or game-like to help people avoid impulse buys or unexamined assumptions about what they really need versus want. So the core AI-assisted mechanism is the mental construction of a complete Ishikawa fishbone diagram before any purchase unlock: users categorize their unexamined assumptions or hidden desire drivers:
 
* **Man (emotional/social pressures)**, 
* **Machine (habits/tools failing)**, 
* **Method (decision processes)**, 
* **Material (physical gaps)**, 
* **Measurement (data/feedback loops)**, and 
* **Mother Nature (environmental/life context)**. 

AI-assistance can weight branches quantitatively, possibly helping to reveal the dominant root cause—often not addressed by the first thoughts or the easy button quick fix purchase ... the real point of these games is to HELP users dig deeper into their true needs versus wants by gamifying what might be a nerve-wracking or tedious process of root cause analysis by provoking more of multi-faceted mindful approach of what is actually needed, ie which is often going to be NOTHING until the situations is thought through. 

### **Category 1: The "Need" Investigator (Full Ishikawa Root Cause Games)**
